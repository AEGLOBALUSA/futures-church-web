## What "coverage" means

The site is built out of **slots** — small named pieces of content you can fill in. Examples:

- The headline at the top of the Kids page
- The paragraph that describes the Selah app
- The "first-visit story" on the Plan-a-Visit page
- Each campus's distinctive paragraph
- Each campus's service times
- Pastor names + photos for each campus

Some slots are **site-wide** (Kids page, Selah page, Generosity paragraph) — usually owned by you (Josh Greenwood or appointee).
Some slots are **campus-specific** — owned by that campus's pastor.

The coverage dashboard shows them all.

---

## What you'll see when you open the coverage page

The page is organized into rows. Each row is a single slot. Each row tells you:

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Page** | Which page on the site this slot lives on (e.g., `/kids`, `/campuses/paradise`) |
| **Slot name** | What this specific piece of content is (e.g., "Program description", "Service times") |
| **Owner** | Who's responsible for filling this in (you, a campus pastor, etc.) |
| **Status** | Empty / Drafted / Filled |
| **Word budget** | Roughly how many words to aim for |
| **Action** | A "Fill →" button that takes you straight to the page in edit mode |

There's also a **summary bar at the top** showing what % of slots are filled across the whole site. Aim for 100%.

## The four ways to use this dashboard

### 1. **Find your own assigned slots** *(Day 1–5 priority)*
Filter or scroll for slots where the owner is `Josh Greenwood (or appointee)`. There are ~13 of these. Knock them out one at a time.

### 2. **See progress per campus** *(Day 7 + Day 10 priority)*
Group/filter by campus. Any campus below 30% on Day 7 → text the pastor. Any at 0% on Day 10 → call the pastor.

### 3. **Spot blockers** *(daily)*
If a slot has been empty for 5+ days and the owner is busy/unresponsive, you might re-assign it to yourself or your writer.

### 4. **Audit at end of week 2** *(Day 14)*
Final pre-launch check. Aim: 18 of 21 campuses ≥ 50% complete, 13 of 17 site-wide slots filled.

## How to fill a slot — step by step

The "Fill →" button is the magic. It does this:

1. You click "Fill →" on the row
2. The site takes you to the actual page where that slot lives — already in **edit mode**, with the slot already highlighted with a warm dashed box
3. You click the dashed box
4. You type
5. Wait 2 seconds — a "saved" indicator appears in the corner
6. Done. Move to the next row.

**You never need to remember which page a slot is on.** The "Fill →" button always knows.

For details on what edit mode looks like and how the dashed boxes work, see `JOSH-3-IN-SITE-EDITING.md`.

## What the colors / status badges mean

You'll see these on the coverage dashboard:

| Badge | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Empty** *(grey)* | No one has typed anything yet |
| **Drafted** *(amber)* | Someone has typed something but it looks short / placeholder |
| **Filled** *(green)* | Looks substantive — meets word budget |

A "Drafted" status doesn't mean it's bad — it just means short. Sometimes a 5-word headline is exactly what's needed and it stays "Drafted" forever. Don't sweat it. Filled vs Empty is what matters.

---

## A note on overwriting

The coverage dashboard shows you a slot's CURRENT value. **Anyone can overwrite anyone.** If you edit a slot that a pastor already filled, you replace their text. There's no merge.

The activity feed (`/intake/admin/activity`) keeps a record of every edit, so you can always see what was there before. But it's not "undo" — you'd have to copy the old value back manually if you wanted to revert.

**In practice:** trust the pastors. Only overwrite if a slot is empty or clearly wrong.
