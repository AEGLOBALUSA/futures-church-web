# Futures College — Photo Casting Brief

This brief governs every face that appears anywhere on the Futures College
landing page. It is a hard filter, not a guideline. If a photo does not pass
the casting standard, it does not ship — even if it matches the slot direction,
even if it is the only available asset.

---

## The casting standard

**Every person on the Futures College page must read as: reasonably good
looking, in shape, visibly on their way in life.**

Three sub-tests, all must pass:

1. **Reasonably good looking.** Composed face, intentional posture, light that
   flatters. Not glamour-shot, but not unflattering either. Real attractive,
   not magazine attractive.
2. **In shape.** Reads as someone who looks after themselves. Not a body-type
   filter — a self-respect filter. Physical evidence of taking life seriously.
3. **On their way in life.** Forward-leaning energy. Eyes engaged, not
   defeated. Looks like they have somewhere to be and something to build. The
   opposite of bewildered, washed-up, or treading water.

Photographs that pass the slot-direction test but fail the casting standard
are **held**, not used.

---

## Why this matters

Futures College sells a future, not a service. The faces on the page are a
proxy for the user's future self twelve months from enrolment. If the faces
look unwell, defeated, or directionless, the user reads: *this is what
graduates of Futures look like.* The page becomes an anti-conversion asset.

Conversely, if the faces read as composed, in-shape, and forward-leaning, the
user reads: *this is who I become.* That is the conversion mechanic.

This is not vanity. It is honesty about what the brand promises.

## Application by surface

### Hero rotation (5 slots)

Each slot maps to an identity question the prospect is asking. The face in the
slot must answer that question with their presence, not just their context.

| Slot | Identity question | Casting note |
|------|-------------------|--------------|
| 01 — student mid-formation | Am I the right age? | Mid-20s subject, present and intent — not zoned out |
| 02 — cohort hallway laugh | Will I belong? | Real laughter between equals — not posed group shot |
| 03 — lecture intensity | Will I be challenged? | Engaged, leaning in — not slumped |
| 04 — mentor moment | Will I be loved? | Eye contact, warmth — not transactional |
| 05 — deployment | Where does this lead? | Graduate visibly leading — composed, not amateur |

### Persona cards (8 slots)

The hardest casting on the page. Each card must show a real person who matches
the archetype AND passes the casting standard. The viewer's test is: *do I
see myself, or do I see someone I want to become?* The right answer is
"both" — the person mirrors the prospect's situation but already carries the
upgrade.

| Persona | Casting note |
|---------|--------------|
| School leaver | 17–19, sharp, composed — not a kid pretending to be serious |
| Parent | Late 30s–40s, well-presented, capable — not exhausted |
| Spiritually restless | Late 20s–30s, alive eyes, urban-modern — not grim |
| Biblically curious | Any age, intent face, Bible visible — not pious |
| Sensing a calling | Any age, forward gaze, weight in the look — not dramatic |
| Builder | 30s–50s, evidence of capability in posture — not corporate cliché |
| Distance learner | Any age, in their own context (home office, café), composed |
| One-subject | Any age, time-poor energy, professional — not casual drop-in |

### Story cards (4 environmental portraits)

Per punch-list §6 — graduates in their current role. Each must look like

### Faculty grid

Faculty must read as people you would learn from — composed, present,
authoritative without being intimidating. Solo portraits only. No combined or
couples shots in the faculty grid.

### Block-level imagery (Block C / G / H / I / J / M / S)

Same standard. Even crowd shots: every visible face in the foreground must
pass. If the lead figure fails the standard, the photo is held regardless of
slot fit.

---

## What never ships

- Stock (Unsplash, Getty, Pexels, etc.) — hard rule, no exceptions
- Photos where the lead subject looks unwell, defeated, or zoned out
- Group photos where the visible faces don't pass the standard
- Posed grins, prayer-hands clichés, fake spontaneity
- Anything taken without permission from the people pictured

---

## Sign-off

Every face photo on the live page must be signed off by the portrait
specialist before it ships. Names + roles must be captioned to defend against
identity confusion (e.g. Ashley vs. Tony, see punch-list P0-5).
